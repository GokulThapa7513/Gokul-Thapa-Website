Introduction

Aimer is a Japanese singer and songwriter who has gained widespread popularity both in Japan and internationally. Her real name is unknown as she prefers to keep her personal life private. Aimer began her career as an independent artist in 2011, releasing her debut single "Rokutosei no Yoru / Kanashimi wa Aurora ni / TWINKLE TWINKLE LITTLE STAR" which caught the attention of fans and industry professionals alike. Since then, she has released multiple albums and singles, and her music has been featured in numerous anime series and films. Known for her emotive vocals and soulful lyrics, Aimer has become a beloved figure in the J-pop music scene and continues to captivate audiences with her unique style and artistry.

Biography

Aimer was born on July 9, 1990, in Kumamoto Prefecture, Japan. Her real name and personal details are not publicly known as she prefers to keep her private life separate from her professional career. She began her music career as an independent artist in 2011, releasing her debut single "Rokutosei no Yoru / Kanashimi wa Aurora ni / TWINKLE TWINKLE LITTLE STAR" in September of that year. The single received critical acclaim and caught the attention of industry professionals, leading to Aimer being signed to Sony Music Japan. In 2012, Aimer released her first major label single, "Re:pray," which was used as the ending theme for the anime series "Bleach." The single reached #2 on the Oricon Weekly Chart, and Aimer began to gain widespread recognition for her music. Since then, Aimer has released multiple albums and singles, including "Sleepless Nights," "Midnight Sun," and "Sun Dance." Her music has been featured in numerous anime series and films, including "Fate/stay night: Unlimited Blade Works," "No. 6," and "Mobile Suit Gundam Unicorn."

She is a frequent guest vocalist for Sawano Hiroyuki (at least 9 collaborations through 2022). From 2011 to 2022, she has had 14 of her 20 singles reach the top 10 Oricon singles charts with two #1s and 3 top 10 singles on the Billboard Download charts. All but 2 of her 6 studio albums have reach the top 10 of the Oricon album charts with the first reaching #11 and the 3rd reaching #18. Four of her compilation albums have reached the top 10 of the Oricon charts. She has participated in at least 20 anime tie-in songs, over a dozen TV drama tie-in songs, and many TV music and other show theme songs. On December 9, 2022, Billboard announced that her single Zankyo Sanka (OP of the anime Demon Slayer: Kimetsu no Yaiba Entertainment District Arc) was named the #1 song of the year on the Billboard JAPAN HOT 100 charts. The song had already been streamed over 200 million times by July 2022. On January 1, 2023, it was announced on her official website that she had married the music creator and producer Tobinai Masahiro.[1]
